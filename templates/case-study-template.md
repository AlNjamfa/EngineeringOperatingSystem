# Case Study Template

## 1. Purpose

This template transforms engineering work into a structured case study that demonstrates engineering judgment, systematic thinking, and continuous improvement.

The purpose of a case study is not to showcase technical complexity.

The purpose is to clearly communicate how a problem was understood, how decisions were made, what tradeoffs were considered, what outcome was achieved, and what lessons were learned.

Every significant engineering project should eventually become a case study.

---

## 2. When To Use

Use this template after:

* Completing a project
* Finishing a major Jira initiative
* Solving a complex debugging problem
* Optimizing cloud costs
* Designing infrastructure
* Building automation
* Improving CI/CD pipelines
* Completing a personal DevOps project
* Creating an architectural improvement

If the work demonstrates engineering judgment, document it.

---

## 3. Instructions

Write for another engineer.

Assume the reader has technical knowledge but no context.

Explain the reasoning behind decisions, not just the implementation.

Focus on clarity, tradeoffs, business value, and lessons learned.

---

# Case Study

## Title

Give the project a clear, descriptive title.

Example:

"Reducing Terraform Deployment Time Through Module Refactoring"

---

## Executive Summary

Summarize the project in three to five sentences.

Answer:

* What was the problem?
* What was done?
* What was the outcome?
* Why did it matter?

This section should be understandable by both engineers and hiring managers.

---

## Business Context

Why did this work matter?

Consider:

* Cost
* Reliability
* Developer productivity
* Operational burden
* Security
* Customer experience
* Time savings
* Business risk

---

## Problem Statement

Describe the problem clearly.

Include:

* Current state
* Desired state
* Gap between the two

Support with evidence where possible.

---

## Initial Understanding

What did I believe at the beginning?

What evidence supported that belief?

What assumptions existed?

---

## Constraints

List relevant constraints.

Examples:

* Time
* Budget
* Existing architecture
* Team knowledge
* Compliance
* Security
* Operational requirements

---

## Investigation

Describe how the problem was explored.

Include:

* Evidence gathered
* Logs reviewed
* Metrics analyzed
* Documentation consulted
* Experiments performed

Explain the investigation process.

## Tradeoff Analysis

Compare the options across:

* Cost
* Reliability
* Complexity
* Maintainability
* Scalability
* Security
* Operational burden

Explain why the chosen option represented the best tradeoff.

---

## Decision

Document:

* Chosen solution
* Why it was selected
* Tradeoffs accepted
* Risks accepted

---

## Implementation

Describe what was actually done.

Focus on engineering reasoning rather than code listings.

Explain the approach, sequence of work, and important decisions.

---

## Validation

How was success measured?

Include:

* Metrics
* Observations
* Testing
* Monitoring
* Performance improvements
* Cost improvements

Describe how the solution was verified.

---

## Outcome

What changed?

Did the project achieve its objective?

Were there unexpected benefits or drawbacks?

---

## Lessons Learned

What did this project teach?

What assumptions changed?

What principle should be remembered?

What would I do differently?

---

## Engineering Judgment

Looking back:

* What was the most important decision?
* What tradeoff mattered most?
* What would a Staff engineer appreciate about this approach?
* What would I challenge if reviewing this six months later?

---

## EOS Improvement

How should the Engineering Operating System improve because of this project?

Examples:

* New framework question
* Better checklist item
* Updated template
* New debugging habit
* Better tradeoff consideration

## Final Reflection

The technical solution solved one problem.

The engineering process improved the engineer.

The greatest value of this project is not only what was built, but the improvement in engineering judgment that resulted from building it.
