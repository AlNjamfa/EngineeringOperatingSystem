# Engineering Case Studies

## Purpose

This directory contains real engineering case studies created using the Engineering Operating System (EOS).

The objective of these case studies is not to demonstrate technical complexity.

The objective is to demonstrate engineering judgment.

Each case study documents the reasoning process behind a technical decision, including problem definition, business context, assumptions, tradeoffs, implementation strategy, validation, and lessons learned.

The emphasis is on **how the engineer thought**, not simply **what the engineer built**.

---

## Why Case Studies Matter

Technology changes rapidly.

Engineering judgment compounds.

A hiring manager can teach a new tool.

It is much harder to teach disciplined reasoning, systematic investigation, cost awareness, ownership thinking, and continuous improvement.

These case studies exist to make that reasoning visible.

---

## Standard Structure

Every case study should follow the same lifecycle.

```text
Problem

↓

Business Context

↓

Current Understanding

↓

Assumptions

↓

Constraints

↓

Investigation

↓

Options

↓

Tradeoff Analysis

↓

Decision

↓

Implementation

↓

Validation

↓

Outcome

↓

Lessons Learned

↓

Engineering Operating System Improvement
```

Every section should explain **why decisions were made**, not simply describe technical implementation.

---

## Engineering Philosophy

The purpose of a case study is not to celebrate success.

The purpose is to improve future engineering judgment.

Failures, incorrect assumptions, and unexpected outcomes are valuable because they expose opportunities to strengthen the Engineering Operating System.

Every case study should leave the repository better than it was before.

---

## Quality Standard

A case study is complete when another engineer can answer:

* What problem existed?
* Why did it matter?
* What assumptions existed?
* What options were considered?
* Why was the chosen solution selected?
* What tradeoffs were accepted?
* How was success validated?
* What lesson should be remembered?
* How should the Engineering Operating System improve because of this work?

If those questions cannot be answered, the case study is incomplete.
